# HPV Vaccine — Myths vs Facts


## Myth 1: The HPV vaccine can treat existing HPV infection or cervical cancer.

**Fact:** The HPV vaccine is **preventive only**. It does not treat existing HPV infections, genital warts, or any form of cervical cancer. Women with existing infections may still benefit from vaccination against other HPV types they have not yet been exposed to.

## Myth 2: The HPV vaccine is only for girls.

**Fact:** HPV affects people of all genders. Boys and men can also get HPV-related cancers (penile, anal, and oropharyngeal cancers) and genital warts. The HPV vaccine is recommended for **both males and females** aged 9–26 years.

## Myth 3: The HPV vaccine is not needed if the person is not sexually active.

**Fact:** The vaccine is **most effective before any exposure to HPV**, which is why vaccination is recommended at ages 9–14. Vaccinating before sexual debut provides the strongest immune response and protection.

## Myth 4: The HPV vaccine is unsafe and causes serious side effects.

**Fact:** HPV vaccines are among the most extensively studied vaccines. They are safe and well-tolerated. Common side effects are mild and temporary (soreness at injection site, mild fever). Serious adverse events are extremely rare. The vaccine is approved by CDSCO in India and endorsed by WHO and ICMR.

## Myth 5: If you have already been vaccinated, you don't need cervical cancer screening.

**Fact:** Vaccination does **not replace** cervical cancer screening (Pap smear / HPV DNA test). Even vaccinated women should follow regular screening schedules as advised by their doctor, because the vaccine does not protect against all HPV types.

## Myth 6: The HPV vaccine can cause infertility.

**Fact:** There is **no scientific evidence** that the HPV vaccine causes infertility. Multiple large-scale studies, including data from India, have found no link between HPV vaccination and fertility problems.

## Myth 7: Natural immunity from HPV infection is better than vaccine-induced immunity.

**Fact:** Natural infection does not reliably produce strong or lasting immunity, and it carries the risk of cancer development. The HPV vaccine produces a **stronger and more consistent immune response** than natural infection and does so safely, without the risks associated with HPV disease.

## Myth 8: The HPV vaccine is only available in private hospitals and is too expensive.

**Fact:** The Government of India has introduced the HPV vaccine into the **Universal Immunisation Programme (UIP)** and it is available **free of charge** for eligible girls aged 9–14 through government health facilities. The indigenously developed **Cervavac** vaccine supports affordable access.

## Myth 9: One dose of the HPV vaccine is sufficient.

**Fact:** For girls aged 9–14, a **2-dose schedule** is recommended (second dose at 6 months). For those aged 15 and above, or those who are immunocompromised, **3 doses** are required (at 0, 1–2, and 6 months). Completing the recommended schedule is important for full protection.

## Myth 10: The HPV vaccine encourages sexual activity in young people.

**Fact:** Research consistently shows that receiving the HPV vaccine does **not** lead to earlier or increased sexual activity. Studies from India and globally confirm there is no behavioural change associated with vaccination. The vaccine is a cancer-prevention measure, not related to sexual behaviour.
